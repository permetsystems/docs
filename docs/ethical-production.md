# Trustless production

The protocol targets a participant-owned system that uses consensus-driven blockchain technology to verify products are made ethically — not merely marketed as such.

## Trustless systems

A trustless apparel system verifies ethical production through:

- **Proof of non-slave labor** — human-rights oracles validating safe workplaces and fair compensation.
- **Proof of location** — production steps tied to verifiable facilities and geography.
- **Open audit** — production process data published for community inspection.

Cloud-based crowdsourced oracles and location protocols can validate working conditions. Normalizing these standards would expose operators who lack proof of ethical labor.

Traditional brands benefiting from exploitation will not voluntarily adopt this. The protocol builds an alternative from the ground up and drives adoption of cryptographic verification as an industry standard.

## Transparent supply chain

Open-sourcing the garment production supply chain means:

- Every step of creation is visible and auditable.
- Greenwashing and obscured labor practices become harder to sustain.
- Creators can audit production, propose improvements, and participate in upside from changes they contribute.

## Secondary markets and consumption

The protocol also addresses consumption incentives:

- Integrated secondary markets where pre-owned pieces retain value across multiple owners.
- Community listing and continuous returns from future sales.
- Products that hold value instead of disposable fast-fashion cycles.
- Dynamic records of a garment's journey — preserving history and value without forcing immediate turnover.

A single piece can live multiple lives; robust secondary markets and aligned incentives reduce the environmental pressure of overproduction.
