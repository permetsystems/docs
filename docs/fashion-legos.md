# Open fashion blocks

Fashion Legos are Permet's model for open-source apparel production: a composable garment library where existing work can be forked, remixed, and built upon.

## Forkable garment library

The protocol provides:

- Open-source, end-to-end apparel production supply chain and tooling.
- On-chain portfolios of garments, materials, and designs that can be forked and recombined.
- A "Github for garments" — creators start from proven bases instead of rebuilding from scratch.

## On-chain design registration

Computer-aided designs register on-chain as vectors and production files. They can then drive printed goods, cut-and-sew, and other manufacturing — with credit and compensation tied to the registered work.

Each unit of creative or production work stays independently attributable so others can build on it while originators retain provenance and profit rights.

## Composable culture

Fashion is combinatorial: ideas are remixed daily. Traditional industry discourages open reuse because attribution and compensation are hard to enforce.

The protocol makes reuse beneficial:

- Collaborators can reference prior work and include originators in profit distribution.
- Creators benefit when their patterns, techniques, or designs are adopted in new projects.
- A pattern or technique reused across many collections can yield ongoing royalties to its originator.

## Open production data

The garment library open-sources production processes and data:

- Materials, methods, and factory choices available for audit.
- Creators can inspect processes, propose improvements, and earn participation in future profit splits for accepted changes.
- Much like security researchers audit open-source code, production can be reviewed and iterated in the open.

## Registries

Curated registries of designers, producers, and suppliers who have successfully used the garment library create network effects — each new project builds on verified prior work, lowering the cost of launching new apparel.
