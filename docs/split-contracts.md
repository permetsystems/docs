# Split system

The split system is how the protocol retains and redistributes value created in making and wearing apparel.

## Problem

In traditional fashion, creators and producers generate most of the value but receive only a fraction — typically a salary with no claim on downstream earnings. Even high-profile collaborators lack guaranteed participation in the upside they create.

## Mechanism

Cryptographic profit sharing replaces informal agreements with enforceable rules:

1. Contributions to a collection — design, pattern, production, community — are recorded as attributable on-chain assets.
2. A split defines how revenue from sales is divided among contributors.
3. Distribution executes according to those rules, including when work is reused in other projects.

## What this unlocks

- **Royalties on reuse** — permissionless inclusion of prior work in new projects, with automatic profit participation for originators.
- **Residual value** — creators need not sell every piece to realize value; ownership and royalties can persist.
- **Transparent portfolios** — verifiable record of commercial participation for future collaborators.
- **Community ownership** — upside shared among designers, makers, communities, and protocol participants — not captured solely by a brand entity.

## Role in the stack

The split system is one of three core concepts alongside open fashion blocks and curated registries. It is the ownership and redistribution layer that makes composable culture economically viable.

Splits do not alone capture full production-process data on-chain; they establish who earns from a project while other protocol modules handle provenance, production transparency, and verification.
